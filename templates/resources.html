Forencics

Forensics is the application of scientific methods and techniques to investigate crimes, analyze digital or physical evidence, and uncover facts. In digital forensics, investigators examine computers, networks, and electronic devices to recover, preserve, and analyze data for legal or security purposes, helping solve cybercrimes and ensure accountability. WAPT

WAPT (Web Application Penetration Testing) is the practice of identifying and exploiting security vulnerabilities in web applications. It involves assessing authentication, authorization, input validation, session management, and data handling to find weaknesses. The goal is to enhance security, prevent attacks, and ensure safe, reliable web application functionality.. For detailed video lecture Click Here

Crypto & Network

Cryptography is the science of securing information using mathematical algorithms, ensuring data confidentiality, integrity, authentication, and non-repudiation. It is used to encrypt messages, secure communications, and protect sensitive data from unauthorized access. Networking is the practice of connecting computers and devices to share resources, communicate, and transfer data efficiently. It involves protocols, routers, switches, and network topologies to maintain secure and reliable data flow.For detailed video lecture Click Here

Devsecops

**DevSecOps** (Development, Security, and Operations) is an approach that integrates security practices directly into the software development lifecycle. It ensures that security is automated and continuous, from coding to deployment, enabling faster, safer releases by addressing vulnerabilities early and maintaining compliance without slowing down development.
